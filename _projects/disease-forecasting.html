Early Disease Forecasting in Dairy Calves

A multimodal artificial intelligence project for predicting disease risk before clinical symptoms become apparent using behavioral, physiological, imaging, and environmental monitoring data.

Project Overview

Disease remains one of the most important challenges affecting calf health, growth performance, welfare, and economic sustainability in dairy production systems. Traditional disease detection often depends on visible clinical signs, which may appear only after physiological and behavioral changes have already occurred.

This project investigates whether multimodal artificial intelligence can identify early indicators of disease risk before clinical symptoms become apparent. By integrating multiple sources of biological, behavioral, and environmental information, the goal is to support proactive intervention and precision health management in pre-weaning dairy calves.

Multimodal Monitoring System

Multiple monitoring systems are integrated to continuously track calf health, feeding behavior, growth, and environmental conditions. These data streams provide complementary information that may reveal disease-related changes before clinical diagnosis.

Feature Engineering

Longitudinal features are generated from multiple data streams to capture behavioral, physiological, and biological changes that may precede disease events.

Behavioral & Growth Features

  • Milk intake and feeding behavior
  • Feeder visit frequency and duration
  • Body weight and growth trajectories
  • Depth-image-derived body measurements

Health & Biological Features

  • Health scores and disease history
  • Temperature and veterinary records
  • Serum IgG and immune-related indicators
  • Microbiome-derived biological features
