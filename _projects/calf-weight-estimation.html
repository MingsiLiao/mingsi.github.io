Computer Vision-Based Body Weight Prediction in Dairy Calves

An automated depth-imaging project for dairy calf body weight estimation using computer vision, YOLOv8 segmentation, machine learning, and longitudinal growth modeling.

Computer Vision Depth Imaging YOLOv8 Machine Learning Growth Monitoring Precision Livestock Farming

Project Overview

Body weight is an important indicator of calf growth, health, feed efficiency, and weaning readiness. However, frequent body weight collection requires labor, animal handling, and access to weighing equipment, which can limit routine monitoring on dairy farms.

This project investigates the use of depth cameras, computer vision, and machine learning to estimate dairy calf body weight automatically and non-invasively. The goal is to support more frequent growth monitoring while reducing manual labor and animal handling stress.

Experimental Setup

A depth camera was mounted above the automatic milk feeder to capture top-view depth videos of calves during feeding. This setup enabled non-contact image acquisition while calves performed normal feeding behavior, providing depth-based body measurements without manual handling.

Automated Data Collection System

Depth videos were collected using an Intel RealSense camera mounted above an automatic milk feeder. The camera captured top-view depth information while calves visited the feeder, enabling automated image acquisition during routine feeding behavior.

The system was designed to support hands-free video collection under practical farm conditions and to generate depth images and depth maps for downstream segmentation, body measurement extraction, and body weight prediction.

Dataset

Two data collection trials were used. One trial included paired depth videos and manually collected body weight records for prediction modeling. A second trial provided automatically collected depth videos for developing and validating deep learning-based segmentation models.

Computer Vision Pipeline

The workflow converts top-view depth videos into calf body measurements. Calf contours are first segmented from depth images, and body metrics such as width, length, average height, volume, and contour area are extracted. These measurements are then used as predictors for body weight estimation.

Segmentation Methods

Two segmentation strategies were evaluated. A threshold-based image processing method was compared with deep learning-based YOLOv8 segmentation models. The deep learning approach was more robust under variable lighting, calf posture, and Holstein coat patterns.

Threshold-Based Segmentation

  • Rule-based image processing using OpenCV
  • HSV transformation and binary thresholding
  • Morphological operations for contour cleaning
  • Lower robustness under poor depth image quality

YOLOv8 Segmentation

  • Deep learning-based calf contour extraction
  • Model variants from YOLOv8n to YOLOv8x
  • Training on manually refined polygon labels
  • Improved segmentation under complex farm conditions

Model Development

Body weight prediction was evaluated using both single-time-point and longitudinal modeling strategies. Extracted body measurements were used to train and compare regression and machine learning models. Longitudinal modeling was also used to account for repeated measurements from the same calf over time.

Single-Time-Point Prediction

  • Linear regression
  • XGBoost regression
  • Five-fold calf-level cross-validation
  • Repeated randomized validation to assess robustness

Longitudinal Prediction

  • Linear regression
  • XGBoost regression
  • Linear mixed models
  • Repeated-measures modeling for calf growth trajectories

Key Findings

YOLOv8-based segmentation improved calf contour extraction compared with threshold-based image processing, enabling more accurate body metric extraction and stronger body weight prediction. Longitudinal modeling further improved growth prediction by accounting for repeated measurements within individual calves.

Research Activities

  • Processing of depth videos and extraction of depth images and depth maps.
  • Development and evaluation of threshold-based and YOLOv8-based segmentation methods.
  • Extraction of calf body metrics including width, length, average height, volume, and contour area.
