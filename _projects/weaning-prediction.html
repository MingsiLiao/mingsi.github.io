Multimodal Weaning Age Prediction in Dairy Calves

An ongoing multimodal AI project integrating feeding behavior, growth records, health monitoring, computer vision, microbiome profiles, and environmental data for precision weaning management.

Multimodal AI Time-Series Analysis Machine Learning Computer Vision Microbiome Precision Livestock Farming

Project Overview

Weaning is one of the most important management transitions in dairy calf development. Conventional weaning decisions are often based on age-based guidelines or limited production records, which may not fully capture individual variation in calf growth, health, and behavioral development.

This project investigates whether multimodal machine learning can integrate information from feeding behavior, growth records, health monitoring, computer vision, microbiome profiles, and environmental data to support more individualized and data-driven weaning decisions.

Multimodal Data Sources

Multiple biological, behavioral, environmental, and management data streams are being integrated to represent calf development before weaning. These data sources provide complementary views of calf growth, feeding adaptation, health status, and biological maturity.

Feeding

Automatic milk feeder records and visit behavior

Growth

Body weight, age, and growth trajectory records

Health

Health scores, temperature records, and veterinary events

Vision

Depth images and image-derived growth features

Microbiome

Fecal and oral microbial community profiles

Environment

Weather and environmental monitoring records

Study Design

The project focuses on integrating longitudinal calf records collected across the pre-weaning period. Complete multimodal records are used to develop prediction models, while larger automated monitoring datasets can support future validation and model expansion.

Feature Engineering

Feature engineering is a central component of this project because each data source captures a different aspect of calf development. The goal is to convert raw longitudinal records into biologically meaningful predictors for weaning readiness.

Behavioral and Growth Features

  • Milk intake and feeding visit patterns
  • Changes in feeding behavior across age
  • Body weight and average daily gain
  • Depth-image-derived body measurements

Health and Biological Features

  • Health scores and disease events
  • Temperature and veterinary records
  • Serum IgG and early-life health indicators
  • Fecal and oral microbiome profiles

Model Development

Several modeling strategies are being evaluated to determine how different data modalities contribute to weaning prediction. The project is designed to compare traditional machine learning approaches with multimodal and time-series learning strategies.

Machine Learning Models

  • Linear and regularized regression models
  • Random forest and gradient boosting models
  • XGBoost-based prediction models
  • Feature importance and model interpretation

Multimodal Learning

  • Modality-specific feature extraction
  • Early and late fusion strategies
  • Time-series representation learning
  • Calf-level prediction and validation

Expected Impact

This project aims to support precision weaning management by integrating multiple sources of biological and behavioral information into a unified prediction framework. The long-term goal is to help identify calves that are developmentally ready for weaning while reducing reliance on one-size-fits-all age-based decisions.
