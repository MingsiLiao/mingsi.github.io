Genetic Association Analysis in Ovine Johne's Disease

Genome-wide association analysis to identify genetic variants associated with Johne's disease susceptibility in sheep using restored DNA from archived tissue samples.

GWAS SNP Analysis Statistical Genomics FFPE DNA Host Genetics Johne's Disease

Project Overview

Johne's disease is a chronic infectious disease of ruminants caused by Mycobacterium avium subsp. paratuberculosis (MAP). Variation in disease progression among infected animals suggests that host genetic factors may influence disease resistance and susceptibility.

This project used restored DNA extracted from archived formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded (FFPE) tissue samples to conduct a retrospective genome-wide association study (GWAS) in sheep. The study demonstrates the feasibility of utilizing archived diagnostic materials for livestock genetic research and disease susceptibility analysis.

Study Design

Sheep were classified as Johne's disease cases or controls based on tissue qPCR, culture, and histopathological evaluation. DNA restoration techniques were applied to FFPE samples before genotyping using the Illumina Ovine SNP50 BeadChip.

Research Questions

  • Can archived FFPE tissue samples be used for genome-wide association studies?
  • Which genetic variants are associated with Johne's disease susceptibility?
  • Which genomic regions may contribute to host resistance against MAP infection?
  • Can retrospective diagnostic samples support livestock genomics research?
  • What biological pathways are linked to disease progression?

Research Contributions

This work demonstrated the successful restoration and utilization of archived FFPE-derived DNA for SNP genotyping and genome-wide association analysis. The study identified candidate genomic regions potentially associated with Johne's disease susceptibility and highlighted the value of archived diagnostic materials for retrospective livestock genomics research.

Research Activities

  • DNA restoration and quality assessment from archived FFPE tissues.
  • SNP genotyping using the Illumina Ovine SNP50 BeadChip.
  • Genome-wide association analysis for Johne's disease susceptibility.
  • Population genetics and quality control analysis.
  • Candidate gene identification and biological interpretation.
  • Scientific writing, figure preparation, and manuscript development.
