AI & Computer Vision

Computer Vision-Based Diarrhea Detection in Dairy Calves

An automated image-based research project for dairy calf diarrhea identification using rear-end images collected at automatic milk feeders, supervised computer vision models, and large vision-language models.

Computer Vision Deep Learning Vision-Language Models Animal Health Precision Livestock Farming

Project Overview

Calf diarrhea is one of the most important health challenges in pre-weaning dairy calves. Traditional identification relies on manual observation, which can be subjective, time-consuming, and difficult to scale in modern dairy operations.

This project investigates the use of computer vision and machine learning techniques for automated diarrhea identification using rear-end images collected during routine visits to automatic milk feeders. The long-term goal is to support earlier, more consistent, and scalable health monitoring in dairy production systems.

Experimental Setup

Rear-end images were collected using cameras installed behind automatic milk feeders in a commercial pre-weaning calf barn. This configuration enables passive image acquisition during normal feeding behavior without requiring additional animal handling.

Dataset

Images were collected under practical farm conditions and manually reviewed to support supervised model development and evaluation. The dataset was curated for both rear-end detection and diarrhea classification tasks.

AI Pipeline

The workflow consists of two primary computer vision tasks. Rear-end regions are first localized from farm-collected images, followed by diarrhea classification using the extracted region of interest. Model outputs can subsequently be integrated into automated calf health monitoring systems.

Key Findings

Research Activities

  • Development of data collection and annotation workflows.
  • Construction and quality control of image datasets collected from commercial dairy operations.
  • Training and evaluation of computer vision models for detection and classification tasks.
  • Benchmarking of large vision-language models under zero-shot, few-shot, and hierarchical prompting settings.
  • Statistical analysis, result visualization, and manuscript preparation.
