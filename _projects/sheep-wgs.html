Sheep Breed Differentiation Using Whole-Genome Sequencing

Project Overview

Royal White and White Dorper sheep are economically important meat-type hair sheep breeds in the United States, but their genome-wide genetic architecture has been poorly characterized. Understanding breed-specific genomic variation can support future breeding decisions, conservation strategies, and selection for economically important traits.

This project used whole-genome sequencing to compare Royal White and White Dorper sheep, identify genome-wide SNPs and indels, evaluate population structure, and detect putative selection signatures associated with traits such as parasite resistance, growth, reproduction, and meat production.

Study Breeds

This study compared two economically important hair sheep breeds, Royal White and White Dorper, using whole-genome sequencing to characterize genetic variation and breed differentiation.

Study Design

Whole-genome sequencing was performed on Royal White and White Dorper ewes from the same flock. High-confidence SNPs and indels were identified, functionally annotated, and used for population structure and selective sweep analyses.

Variant Discovery

Genome-wide SNPs and small insertions and deletions were identified separately for each breed. Variant annotation was used to characterize predicted functional effects and prioritize genes with high or moderate impact variants.

Population Genomics

Population structure was evaluated using principal component analysis and discriminant analysis of principal components. These analyses revealed low but detectable genetic differentiation between Royal White and White Dorper sheep.

Research Questions

  • What genome-wide variants distinguish Royal White and White Dorper sheep?
  • How do the two breeds differ in population structure?
  • Which genomic regions show evidence of recent positive selection?
  • Which candidate genes are associated with parasite resistance, growth, reproduction, and production traits?
  • How can whole-genome sequencing support future genomic selection in hair sheep breeds?

Key Findings

Whole-genome sequencing revealed breed-specific SNPs, indels, and putative selection signatures in Royal White and White Dorper sheep. Candidate regions were associated with economically important traits, including parasite resistance, immune response, growth, reproduction, meat quality, and production efficiency.

Research Activities

  • Processing and quality control of whole-genome sequencing data.
  • Read alignment and variant calling for SNPs and indels.
  • Functional annotation of breed-specific genetic variants.
  • Population structure analysis using PCA and DAPC.
  • Selective sweep analysis using FST, nucleotide diversity, and Tajima's D.
  • Candidate gene interpretation using QTL and functional annotation resources.
  • Scientific writing, data visualization, and manuscript preparation.
